## Executive Summary

Tornado Cash is a fully decentralized, non-custodial cryptocurrency mixer protocol using zk-SNARKs for privacy on Ethereum and EVM-compatible chains. Founded in August 2019 by Roman Storm, Alexey Pertsev, and Roman Semenov, the protocol was sanctioned by OFAC in August 2022 for allegedly laundering over $7 billion, including funds stolen by North Korea's Lazarus Group. In a landmark ruling, the Fifth Circuit Court found OFAC exceeded its authority in November 2024, and sanctions were officially lifted in March 2025. However, criminal proceedings against the founders continue.

### 1. Legal Status (100% Verified)

**OFAC Sanctions Timeline**:
- **August 8, 2022**: OFAC sanctioned Tornado Cash for laundering $7B+ since 2019
- **Reason**: Lazarus Group (North Korean state hackers) laundered $455M through protocol
- **Authority**: Executive Order 13694 (as amended)

**Fifth Circuit Ruling**:
- **November 26, 2024**: 3-0 panel ruled OFAC exceeded statutory authority
- **Key Finding**: Immutable smart contracts are NOT "property" under IEEPA
- **Rationale**: Contracts lack ownership, control, and exclusivity characteristics

**Sanctions Lifted**:
- **March 21, 2025**: OFAC officially delisted Tornado Cash
- **Reason**: Following court ruling; acknowledged "novel legal and policy issues"
